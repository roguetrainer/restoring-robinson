# Found Audio Files of Joan Robinson

This document tracks verified audio sources used for training the voice model.

## Primary Source: The Stanford Lectures (May 1974)
**Host:** Professor Robert Marks' Personal Archive
**Quality:** Moderate to Low (requires restoration).
**Link:** [Robert Marks Archive](https://www.bobm.net.au/iows/jrobinson.html)

* **File 1:** "What is Wrong with Neoclassical Economics?" (Parts 1 & 2)
* **File 2:** "Traditional Economics is Inappropriate for Developing Economies" (Parts 1 & 2)
* **File 3:** "Socialist Economies and Consumer Sovereignty"

## Secondary Source: The Dunning Trust Lectures (March 1974)
**Host:** Queen's University Digital Collection
**Quality:** Moderate (University master tapes).
**Link:** [Queen's University Dunning Trust](https://www.queensu.ca/dunning-trust/joan-v-robinson-1973-1974)

* **File 1:** "Growth and the Economist" (March 25, 1974)
* **File 2:** "Doomsday is Today" (March 27, 1974)

## Archival Notes
* **Training Strategy:** Use the cleanest segments from the Queen's University lectures for *Voice Cloning* (learning the timbre).
* **Content Strategy:** Use the Stanford lectures for *Transcription* (learning the text) to re-generate those specific famous arguments.
